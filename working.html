Working of Airplane !!

How Wings Lift the Plane ?

Airplane wings are shaped to make air move faster over the top of the wing. When air moves faster, the pressure of the air decreases. So the pressure on the top of the wing is less than the pressure on the bottom of the wing. The difference in pressure creates a force on the wing that lifts the wing up into the air.

Sir Isaac Newton proposed three laws of motion in 1665. These Laws of Motion help to explain how a planes flies. 1. If an object is not moving, it will not start moving by itself. If an object is moving, it will not stop or change direction unless something pushes it. 2. Objects will move farther and faster when they are pushed harder. 3. When an object is pushed in one direction, there is always a resistance of the same size in the opposite direction.
